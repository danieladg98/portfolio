CLOSER TO BANKSY

Multimedia Design

“Closer to Banksy” is an interactive installation that aims to connect the audience with the essence of the artist and his own art. It is a multisensory and reactive experience, based on one of his last interventions “Love is in the Bin”.

While the users remain in the “collective space”, the artwork remains faithful to the original. When a user enters the delimited corridor, he passes to the “individual space”. As he approaches the wall, where the artwork is projected, the same projection begins to simulate the “self-destruction” that Banksy created with “Love is in the Bin.” This happens because the individual approach represents what the artist fights against: the attempt to possess what belongs to the public.

This antithesis is the basis for the interaction created in this installation: what is considered to be an approximation to the artwork is in reality its alienation. The unpredictability of Banksy is also portrayed in this installation by the randomness with which his quotes appear in the background.

“Closer to Banksy” can be found in Barry Cawston's "Banksy, Dismaland and Others" exibithion at Porto, Portugal.
